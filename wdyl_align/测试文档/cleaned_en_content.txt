Methodology:
This was an open-label, dose-escalating study to assess safety, tolerability, PK, PD, and immunogenicity of ALXN1850 when given IV and SC to adults with HPP.
Study participants were enrolled into 3 cohorts (ALXN1850 15 mg, 45 mg, and 90 mg) in a sequential fashion. An SRC convened after the first participant in each cohort had completed the first IV dose to assess safety and tolerability, and to endorse initiation of SC study drug administration to the first participant as well as IV and SC study drug administration to the remaining participants in the cohort. Once at least 3 participants in each of the first 2 cohorts
(15 mg and 45 mg) had received a single IV dose and 3 qw SC doses, the SRC convened again to review safety and tolerability data, and to determine if the final dose escalation (90 mg) could proceed. After the SRC endorsed each dose escalation, the next higher cohort began dosing at the next dosing level. Cohorts 2 and 3 began dosing after the SRC had reviewed all relevant safety, tolerability, and PK data (for Cohort 3 dose escalation only) from participants in Cohort 1 or Cohorts 1 and 2, respectively, and endorsed dose escalation.
Participants who were positive for ADA at the last assessment of the study were given the option to enroll in the Global HPP Registry to enable further monitoring of their ADA values after the end of the study.
Number of Participants (Planned and Analyzed):
Approximately 15 eligible participants (approximately 5 in each Cohorts 1, 2, and 3), were planned to be enrolled to allow for at least 9 participants (3 per cohort) to “complete” the study. A total of 23 participants were screened and provided informed consent, and 15 participants were dosed and included in the safety, PK, PD, and immunogenicity analysis sets.
Diagnosis and Main Criteria for Inclusion and Exclusion:
Male and nonpregnant female participants at least 18 years of age with a clinical diagnosis of HPP and in otherwise good general health. All participants provided written informed consent.
Study Interventions, Dose, Mode of Administration, and Lot Number(s): ALXN1850 SC qw over 3 weeks (a total of 3 doses) at 15, 45, or 90 mg ALXN1850 IV single dose at 15, 45, or 90 mg
IMP lot number: GCS519454
Duration of Study Intervention:
The planned study duration for each participant was up to 22.5 weeks (approximately 5 months) in total. The first participant in each cohort had a duration of up to 155 days (approximately
5 months). The screening period was up to 56 days, the treatment period was 65 days, and the follow-up period was up to 34 days. Subsequent participants in each cohort had a duration of 148 days (approximately 5 months). The screening period was up to 56 days, the treatment period was 57 days, and the follow-up period was up to 35 days.
Summary of Results and Conclusions:
Demography and Baseline Characteristics:
There were 4 male participants (26.7%) and 11 female participants (73.3%) dosed in the study. The median age was 47.0 years (range 31 to 57 years). All 15 participants were White and not Hispanic or Latino.
Exposure:
All participants received one IV dose and 3 SC doses with the exception of 1 participant in Cohort 2 that received only 1 of 3 SC doses and 2 participants in Cohort 3 that received 2 of 3 SC doses.

Conclusions:
ALXN1850 given IV as a single dose and given SC in 3 qw doses had an acceptable safety profile and was well tolerated in adults with HPP.
No deaths were reported during the study.
Injection site reactions (10 occurrences) were reported for 8 participants (53.3%) after receiving ALXN1850 via SC injection. No adverse events of hypersensitivity or anaphylactic reaction were reported during the study.
Following IV and SC doses, the peak and total exposure of ALXN1850 increased with increasing dose in a greater than dose proportional manner over the dose range of 15 to 90 mg. Mean half-life of ALXN1850 was 72.6 hours and 140 hours following IV and SC administration, respectively. Statistical analysis of trough concentrations demonstrated attainment of steady state on Day 8.
Absolute bioavailability of ALXN1850 following SC dose ranged from 28.6% to 36.7%.
The concentrations of PPi, PLP, and PLP/PL ratio decreased following IV dose and over time returned to baseline levels. The magnitude and duration of ALXN1850 induced reductions in PPi, PLP, and PLP/PL ratio appear dose dependent. The maximum lowering effects occurred after IV dose in 7 days, and lowering effects were sustained for 3 to 4 weeks post last SC dose prior to returning to baseline.
Treatment-emergent positive ADA response did not have apparent impact on ALXN1850 PK or PD, and were not associated with hypersensitivity, anaphylaxis, or any other immune mediated adverse event.

INTRODUCTION
Hypophosphatasia is a rare, inherited, metabolic disease caused by deficient activity of the TNSALP, typically due to loss of function mutation(s) in the ALPL gene. As a direct result of deficient TNSALP, progressive damage to vital organs along with other clinical sequelae may occur, including deformity and softness of bones (rickets-like bone deformities and osteomalacia), fractures and pseudofractures, pain, profound muscle weakness, respiratory failure (primarily in infants), seizures (mainly in infants), impaired renal function, impaired mobility, and dental abnormalities (Berkseth et al 2013; Rockman-Greenberg 2013; Weber et al 2016; Whyte et al 2013). Asfotase alfa (STRENSIQ®) is currently the only approved treatment for patients with pediatric-onset HPP.
ALXN1850 is an investigational ERT that addresses the underlying cause of HPP by replacing the defective enzyme, thus reversing the mineralization defects of the skeleton and improving systemic manifestations of HPP. ALXN1850 is a human recombinant
TNSALP-Fc-deca-aspartate fusion protein. It is a soluble glycoprotein comprised of
2 polypeptide chains of 724 amino acids made from the catalytic domain of human TNSALP (SwissProt, P05186), the human Ig G2/4 Fc domain (SwissProt P01859 and Genebank K01316; to facilitate purification and extend half-life), and a deca aspartate peptide (to target the bone).
The purpose of this FIH, open-label, dose-escalating study was to assess the safety and tolerability, PK and PD parameters, and immunogenicity of ALXN1850 when given IV and SC to adult participants with HPP. This FIH study was designed to support an efficient and tolerable pharmacologically effective dose in participants with HPP for whom ALXN1850 has been designed to deliver the required enzyme with less frequent SC administration than asfotase alfa, and therefore reduce the burden of treatment for this group of patients.

INVESTIGATIONAL PLAN
Overview of Study Design
This was an open-label, dose-escalating study to assess safety, tolerability, PK, PD, and immunogenicity of ALXN1850 when given IV and SC to adults with HPP.
Study participants were enrolled into 3 cohorts (ALXN1850 15 mg, 45 mg, and 90 mg) in a sequential fashion. An SRC convened after the first participant in each cohort had completed the first IV dose to assess safety and tolerability, and to endorse initiation of SC study drug administration to the first participant as well as IV and SC study drug administration to the remaining participants in the cohort. Once at least 3 participants in each of the first 2 cohorts
(15 mg and 45 mg) had received a single IV dose and 3 qw SC doses, the SRC convened again to review safety and tolerability data, and to determine if the final dose escalation (90 mg) could proceed. After the SRC endorsed each dose escalation, the next higher cohort began dosing at the next dosing level. Cohorts 2 and 3 began dosing after the SRC had reviewed all relevant safety, tolerability, and PK data (for Cohort 3 dose escalation only) from participants in Cohort 1 or Cohorts 1 and 2, respectively, and endorsed dose escalation.
Participants who were positive for ADA at the last assessment of the study were given the option to enroll in the Global HPP Registry to enable further monitoring of their ADA values after the end of the study.

Discussion of Study Design
As this was the first time ALXN1850 was dosed in humans, an open-label dose-escalating design was chosen for initial assessment of the safety, tolerability, PK, and PD of ALXN1850 IV and SC in adults with HPP. The scientific rationale for each aspect of the study design included the following:
This study was conducted in adult participants with HPP because ALXN1850 was designed as a TNSALP ERT that was expected to lead to improvement in this patient population. Because of the potential for immunogenicity, this study enrolled adults with HPP who were not anticipated to require continued treatment with ERT, based on their signs and symptoms, and the investigator’s clinical assessment of their current condition.
A single ALXN1850 IV dose provided the reference necessary for assessing absolute bioavailability.
A weekly SC dosing provided the PK and PD information that supported the intended dose regimen in this patient population.
Three weekly doses of SC ALXN1850 provided accumulation information for this study drug.
The qw SC dose range proposed for this study (15 to 90 mg) was expected to cover the therapeutic range in humans.
An 8-week follow-up provided enough time to assess immunogenicity and possible risks to the participants.

Changes Following Study Database Lock and Post-hoc Analyses
Changes made post-database lock are described below:
Injection/infusion site reactions noted on the injection/infusion site evaluation form that were not captured as AEs are included in discussion of injection site reactions.
Calculation of absolute bioavailability of ALXN1850 following SC administration was modified using AUCtau value of the third SC dose (instead of the first SC dose) versus AUCinf value of the IV administration.

Adverse Events
Adverse events are summarized overall and by cohort as well as by treatment; however, the number of participants was small and therefore comparisons between dose levels and treatments must be made with caution. Number of participants (N) is too small to draw conclusions.
Brief Summary of Adverse Events
Overall, 12 participants (80.0%) experienced any TEAE during the study; 9 participants (60.0%) experienced 13 TEAEs after IV dosing and 12 participants (80.0%) experienced 33 TEAEs after SC dosing. TEAEs were reported for 3 participants (60.0%) in Cohort 1, 5 participants (100%) in Cohort 2, and 4 participants (80.0%) in Cohort 3. One TEAE in a participant in Cohort 1 was not included in the TEAEs count due to an incomplete AE start date and therefore the event could not be assigned to treatment with IV or SC administration. While the number of participants with events was not affected, the number of TEAEs and related TEAEs for Cohort 1 and overall does not reflect this event.
No deaths were reported during the study. One participant (6.7%) from Cohort 3 experienced a TESAE (atrial fibrillation) which was considered by the investigator as not related to study drug; a narrative for the event is presented in Section 5.1.1.4. No participants experienced TEAEs leading to withdrawal of study drug.
A total of 10 participants (66.7%) experienced TEAEs considered related to ALXN1850. Treatment-related TEAEs were reported for 3 participants (60.0%) in Cohort 1, 5 participants
(100%) in Cohort 2, and 2 participants (40.0%) in Cohort 3. A participant with multiple occurrences of a TEAE is counted only once at the highest relationship.
Site reactions occurred with 10 SC injection administrations of ALXN1850 for 8 participants with a total of 13 events.
Five participants reported a total of 9 AEs, and an additional 3 participants had reactions captured on the injection/infusion site evaluation form that were not reported as AEs. In addition, 1 participant with a reported AE had an additional reaction noted on the injection/infusion site evaluation form that was not reported as an AE.
There was 1 participant who reported an injection site reaction with all 3 SC injections (total of 5 AEs reported); the remaining 7 participants had an injection site reaction only with 1 injection with only 1 participant reporting 2 events at the injection site (total of 8 events of which 4 were reported as AEs).
Following medical review by the Sponsor, no systemic reactions within the 24-hour period following SC study drug administration. There were no reports consistent with hypersensitivity reactions or anaphylaxis following IV study drug administration. Evaluation of reactions following SC study drug administration is further discussed in Section 5.1.1.6).
Following IV administration of ALXN1850, mild induration, not reported as an AE, was observed for 1 participant as recorded on the injection/infusion site evaluation form. Following
medical review by the Sponsor, 1 participant was identified with mild headache, with onset within 24 hours of study drug infusion, that was considered consistent with a possible infusion associated reaction. There were no other reports consistent with hypersensitivity reactions or anaphylaxis following IV study drug administration. Evaluation of these events is further discussed in Section 5.1.1.6.

A total of 10 participants (66.7%) experienced 29 TEAEs considered by the investigator to be related to study drug. Treatment-related TEAEs were reported for 3 participants (60.0%) in Cohort 1, 5 participants (100%) in Cohort 2, and 2 participants (40.0%) in Cohort 3.

Treatment-Emergent Serious Adverse Events
One TESAE was reported for a participant in Cohort 3 (Table 14.3.1.5, Table 14.3.1.6, and Listing 14.3.2.1).
Participant 01500017 (Cohort 3), a 57-year-old White female with hypophosphatasia, received a 90 mg ALXN1850 IV dose on Day 1 and 90 mg ALXN1850 SC dose on Day 8, 15, and 22. The participant had no other relevant medical history.
On Day 50, 28 days after the last dose of study drug, the participant experienced a TESAE of grade 3 atrial fibrillation that resulted in hospitalization. It was reported that just before arrival to the emergency room, the participant got out of bed quickly, felt nauseated with sudden syncope, collapsed with severe intensity, and lost consciousness. The participant experienced chills, weakness, and sacral pain. The participant had orthostatic hypotension upon emergency room admission and also had slightly elevated creatinine. On the same day, the participant was hospitalized for observation and an ECG showed atrial fibrillation with a rapid ventricular response. A repeat ECG showed a normal sinus rhythm. An echocardiogram showed normal systolic function, ejection fraction of 60%, trace mitral regurgitation, and trace tricuspid valve regurgitation.
On Day 51, ECG showed an ejection fraction of 60% with trace mitral regurgitation with no other significant abnormality. The event was considered resolved on Day 51 and the participant was discharged from the hospital. The participant was given acetylsalicylic acid for the event of atrial fibrillation. The investigator considered the TESAE not related to the study drug.

Following single dose IV administration of ALXN1850 to adults with HPP, the mean plasma concentration versus nominal time profiles for the first participant and subsequent participants in each cohort (15, 45, and 90 mg dose) were stable and followed a similarly parallel trend without clearly discernible distribution phase. Time to attain peak plasma concentration of ALXN1850 (the median estimate) was noted at 2.00, 0.60, and 0.72 hours for Cohorts 1, 2, and 3, respectively, relative to the 0.5-hour IV infusion duration. Thereafter, concentrations declined gradually reaching close to below the limit of quantification by 336 hours (the last sample collected) for the first participants while, for the subsequent participants, the last sample after IV infusion was collected at 168 hours without reaching hour-336.
Following 3 SC doses (administered post IV dose) at 1-week intervals, the mean plasma concentration versus nominal time profiles for the first and the subsequent participants in each cohort (15, 45, and 90 mg dose) were also stable and generally followed a similarly parallel trend. Time to attain peak plasma concentration of ALXN1850 (the median estimate) following the SC dose 1 for Cohorts 1, 2, and 3 for all the participants was around 48 hours. The corresponding values following SC doses 2 and 3 were around 24 hours and between 36 and
48 hours, respectively. The last sample after SC dose 1 and SC dose 2 was collected at 168 hours post SC doses and concentrations are much higher than the limit of quantification.

From the study results, ADA apparently did not have impact on the PK parameters of ALXN1850; however, the number of participants that have treatment-emergent ADA response is limited (N = 1) and there was no detectible NAb positivity.
Following IV dose, for all participants (first and subsequent combined), the arithmetic mean Cmax (peak plasma exposure) of ALXN1850 was 5.48, 13.8, and 33.5 µg/mL and the arithmetic mean AUCinf (total plasma exposure) was 427, 1030, and 2550 h*µg/mL for Cohorts 1, 2, and 3, respectively. Mean half-life of ALXN1850 in all participants (first and subsequent combined) was 72.6 hours.
Following SC dose 1, for all participants (first and subsequent combined), the arithmetic mean Cmax (peak plasma exposure) of ALXN1850 was 0.925, 3.28, and 9.12 µg/mL and the arithmetic mean AUCtau (total plasma exposure) was 122, 431, and 1200 h*µg/mL for Cohorts 1, 2, and 3, respectively. Immunogenicity
A summary of ALXN1850 ADA status and NAb response is presented in Table 14.3.4.8.1 and ADA titer by relative time to dose is presented in Table 14.3.4.8.2. Mean ADA titer versus relative time to dose and individual ADA titer versus relative time to dose are presented by cohort in Figure 14.2.3.1 and Figure 14.2.3.2, respectively. A total of 4 (26.7%) participants had ADA positive status, including 3 (20.0%) with pre-existing immunogenicity and 1 (6.7%) with treatment-emergent ADA response. All ADA positive participants exhibited low titers (range: 10 to 80). No participants had NAb positive results. Treatment-emergent positive ADA response did not have apparent impact on ALXN1850 PK or PD, and were not associated with hypersensitivity, anaphylaxis, or any other immune mediated AE. No participants in the 45 mg and 90 mg dose groups exhibited treatment-emergent ADA responses, suggesting higher doses may result in lower incidence of immunogenicity.
Individual ADA status, NAb response, and maximum ADA titer are presented for all participants and for ADA positive participants only in Listing 16.2.9.2.1 and Listing 16.2.9.2.2, respectively. CONCLUSIONS
ALXN1850 given IV as a single dose and given SC in 3 qw doses had an acceptable safety profile and was well tolerated in adults with HPP.
No deaths were reported during the study.
Injection site reactions (10 occurrences) were reported for 8 participants (53.3%) after receiving ALXN1850 via SC injection. No adverse events of hypersensitivity or anaphylactic reaction were reported during the study.
Following IV and SC doses, the peak and total exposure of ALXN1850 increased with increasing dose in a greater than dose proportional manner over the dose range of 15 to 90 mg. Mean half-life of ALXN1850 was 72.6 hours and 140 hours following IV and SC administration, respectively. Statistical analysis of trough concentrations demonstrated attainment of steady state on Day 8.
Absolute bioavailability of ALXN1850 following SC dose ranged from 28.6% to 36.7%.
The concentrations of PPi, PLP, and PLP/PL ratio decreased following IV dose and over time returned to baseline levels. The magnitude and duration of ALXN1850 induced reductions in PPi, PLP, and PLP/PL ratio appear dose dependent. The maximum lowering effects occurred after IV dose in 7 days, and lowering effects were sustained for 3 to 4 weeks post last SC dose prior to returning to baseline.
Treatment-emergent positive ADA response did not have apparent any impact on ALXN1850 PK or PD, and were not associated with hypersensitivity, anaphylaxis, or any other immune mediated adverse event.
